What to draw

Please use your mouse to mark the areas of the image that belong to the specified part of the central person with foreground lines and the rest with background lines

The specified part name (you can find it in the yellow top line) is named as if you were the person. So if the task is to mark the left arm then imagine you are the person in the image and mark your left arm. Little red dots additionally are drawn onto the specified part.

Please, mark only those areas of the image where you can actually see the specified part.

Everything that a person wears should be included with the person. All other things -- including sports gear like bats, balls -- that you would not count as a cloth should not be included. See some examples below.

Add more scribbles with the use of your mouse until only parts of the image appear brighter that belong to the specified part of the central person. Person scribbles are shown as solid blue lines and non-person scribbles as solid green lines.

Please work on all images to be able to submit the task.


Good examples

Carefully scribble around the right arm.

Mark all other parts of the person with green background scribbles.

Sometimes only small portions of the part are visible.


Bad examples

We will reject HITs that do not follow these instructions.

Please, add all areas of the specified part. For example do not forget hands.

Try to exclude all other areas of the person. In this example the foot should not be included and should be marked with a green background scribble.


How to draw

Click and hold your left mouse button to produce a line that marks part of the image that belong to the inside of a part of the person. Release the button to stop drawing.

Use the right button to mark outside parts.

Wait for a moment after each line until the display is updated. Add more scribbles until only parts of the image containing the specified part of the central person appear brighter.

Switch mask on/off to see better

By pressing T or the button "Hide/Show Mask" you can switch the mask on and off. Sometimes this makes it easier to see the inside and the outside of the central person.

You can add scribbles no matter if the mask is switched on or off.

Zoom in to see better

Scroll with the mouse wheel to zoom in. Click "Reset Zoom" or press escape to reset back to normal.


Move around in the image

Press 'space' and drag with the mouse to move around in the image (panning). You can also move press the arrow keys to move.


Other controls

Hide/Show Mask
"Hide/Show Mask" or press T.
Undo
If you make a mistake, click "Undo" or press Ctrl-Z.
Redo
You can also re-do an action with Ctrl-Y.
Clear
Clear and start from the beginning.
Reset Zoom
When zoomed in, you you can click "Reset Zoom" to zoom out again.
Previous Image
Press "Previous Image" to get to the previous image.
Next Image
Press "Next Image" to get to the next image.


Why are we doing this?

We are building a database of segmented people. This is part of ongoing research at Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems.
