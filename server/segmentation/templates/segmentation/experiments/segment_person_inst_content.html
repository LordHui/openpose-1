What to draw

Please use your mouse to mark the part of the image that belong to the central person with foreground lines and the rest with background lines

Everthing that a person wears should be included with the person. All other things -- including sports gear like bats, balls -- that you would not count as a cloth should not be included. See some examples below.

Add more scribbles with the use of your mouse until only parts of the image appear brighter that belong to the central person. Person scribbles are shown as solid blue lines and non-person scribbles as solid green lines.

Please work on all images to be able to submit the task.

How to draw

Click and hold your left mouse button to produce a line that marks part of the image that belong to the inside of the person. Relase the button to stop drawing.

Use the right button to mark outside parts.

Wait for a moment after each line until the display is updated. Add more scribbles until only parts of the image containing the central person appear brighter.

Switch mask on/off to see better

By pressing T or the button "Hide/Show Mask" you can switch the mask on and off. Sometimes this makes it easier to see the inside and the outside of the central person.

You can add scribbles no matter if the mask is switched on or off.

The mask is switched on.

The mask is switch off.


Zoom in to see better

Scroll with the mouse wheel to zoom in. Click "Reset Zoom" or press escape to reset back to normal.


Move around in the image

Press 'space' and drag with the mouse to move around in the image (panning). You can also move press the arrow keys to move.


Other controls

Hide/Show Mask
"Hide/Show Mask" or press T.
Undo
If you make a mistake, click "Undo" or press Ctrl-Z.
Redo
You can also re-do an action with Ctrl-Y.
Reset Zoom
When zoomed in, you you can click "Reset Zoom" to zoom out again.
Previous Image
Press "Reset Zoom" to get to the previous image.
Next Image
Press "Reset Zoom" to get to the next image.


Why are we doing this?

We are building a database of segmented people. This is part of ongoing research at Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems.
